<p><p>The biological fluorophore market includes types such as Green Fluorescent Protein (GFP), Phycoerythrin, and Allophycocyanin, each used for various applications in molecular biology and biochemistry. GFP, derived from jellyfish, emits bright green fluorescence and is widely used for gene expression studies. Phycoerythrin, found in red algae, offers high fluorescence and sensitivity, making it ideal for flow cytometry. Allophycocyanin, another red pigment, is prized for its stability and efficiency, often utilized in multiplex assays and other advanced analytical techniques.</p></p>
