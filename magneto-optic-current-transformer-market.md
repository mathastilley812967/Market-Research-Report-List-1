Fiber Type MOCT employs optical fibers for sensing and transmitting electrical currents, offering advantages such as reduced weight, immunity to electromagnetic interference, and enhanced accuracy, making them suitable for modern grid applications. In contrast, Non-Fiber Type MOCT utilizes conventional materials without fiber optics, generally serving traditional applications where cost-efficiency is paramount. Both types cater to various sectors, emphasizing reliability and performance in current measurement.</p></p>
