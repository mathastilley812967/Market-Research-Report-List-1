<p><p>Bicycle-sharing systems can be categorized into three main market types. </p><p>Point-to-point systems allow users to pick up a bike at one location and drop it off at another, facilitating one-way trips. </p><p>Ride-and-return models require users to return the bike to its original station, promoting circular usage and station density. </p><p>Distributed markets feature an open system with bikes dispersed across various locations, enabling flexible access but requiring efficient management to balance bike availability. Each type caters to different user needs and urban environments.</p></p>
