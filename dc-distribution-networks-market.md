<p><p>DC distribution networks are classified into three market types based on voltage levels: low voltage (LV), medium voltage (MV), and high voltage (HV). Low voltage networks typically serve residential and light commercial areas, facilitating direct connections for appliances and renewable energy sources. Medium voltage networks cater to industrial applications, providing a balance between efficiency and capacity. High voltage networks handle long-distance power transmission, ensuring minimal losses and the ability to connect large generation sources, like solar farms, to the grid.</p></p>
