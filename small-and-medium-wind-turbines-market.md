<p><p>The small and medium wind turbine market is primarily divided into two types: Horizontal-Axis Wind Turbines (HAWT) and Vertical-Axis Wind Turbines (VAWT). HAWTs, featuring blades mounted on a horizontal rotor, are most common and typically offer higher efficiency and power output. In contrast, VAWTs have blades that rotate around a vertical axis, allowing for versatility in wind direction and easier maintenance, often making them suitable for urban or constrained environments. Both types cater to diverse energy needs and applications.</p></p>
