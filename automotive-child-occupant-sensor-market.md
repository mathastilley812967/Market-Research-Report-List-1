<p><ul><li>Ultrasonic</li><li>Electric Field</li><li>Capacitive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive child occupant sensor market encompasses various technologies used to detect the presence of children in vehicles. Ultrasonic sensors utilize sound waves to measure proximity and determine occupancy. Electric field sensors create a field around the seating area, detecting movements or objects within it. Capacitive sensors rely on changes in capacitance caused by the presence of a child, providing accurate detection. Each type offers distinct advantages in terms of sensitivity, accuracy, and integration within vehicle systems, enhancing safety for young passengers.</p></p>
