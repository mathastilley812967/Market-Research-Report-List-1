<p><p>The laboratory-grown diamond market primarily comprises two types: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) diamonds and High-Pressure High-Temperature (HPHT) diamonds. CVD diamonds are created using a process that deposits carbon atoms onto a substrate, leading to crystal growth, offering excellent clarity and design flexibility. In contrast, HPHT diamonds replicate natural diamond formation by applying high pressure and temperature, resulting in stones that often display unique characteristics. Both types cater to various applications in jewelry and industrial sectors, emphasizing ethical sourcing.</p></p>
