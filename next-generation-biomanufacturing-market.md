<p><p>Next-Generation Biomanufacturing encompasses two key market segments: upstream and downstream biomanufacturing. Upstream biomanufacturing involves processes related to cell culture, fermentation, and initial production stages, where raw materials are converted into biological products. This stage focuses on optimizing conditions for maximum yield and productivity. Conversely, downstream biomanufacturing deals with purification, formulation, and packaging of the final product. This phase ensures that the biological products meet quality standards and are ready for distribution, emphasizing efficiency and cost-effectiveness in processing.</p></p>
